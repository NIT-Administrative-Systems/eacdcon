Present at EACDcon

At the request-for-proposal stage of planning EACDcon, we are soliciting rough ideas for talks until January 30th, 2024.

You do not need a ready-made talk to submit a proposal. All you need is a subject and a rough idea of how long you want to talk. There are two sizes of timeslot: 15-minute lightning talks, and longer 30-minute talks. You can present solo, or you can present jointly with a few other team members.

Your proposed talk should be relevant to EACD's work. It does not necessarily need to be about a technology or project that we're currently working on, or even entirely tech-related. The sorts of things we're looking for are:

  • Demonstration of something you've worked on recently that you're proud of
  • Case study on a specific problem (example), how you figured it out, and how it was remediated
  • Alternative tool choice that we could adopt & how we'd use it to improve
  • Anything that you think might benefit the rest of the team!

Every proposal may not be accepted. We will strive to include everybody, but time is a precious resource, and we may not be able to fit every proposal into a single afternoon! You will be notified if your talk was accepted by February 7th, 2024. Please hold off on working on your presentation until then!

If your proposal is accepted, you will be expected to prepare a slide deck, demo, or another similar visual artifact for your talk. We ask that you spend no more than 4-5 hours preparing your presentation.
